Valentino Rossi VR46(The Doctor)

Valentino Rossi, also known as “The Doctor,” is an Italian Motorcycle Grand Prix (GP) racer and a multi-time MotoGP, 125, 250, and 500 cc Grand Prix World Champion. Many consider Valentino to be one of the greatest motorcycle road racers of all time. He has won nine Grand Prix World Championships, including seven in the current premier class MotoGP and one 500 cc World Championship in the previous premier class. Rossi is the only rider to have competed in 400 or more Grands Prix in both the 500 cc GP and MotoGP. He also owns Sky Racing Team VR46, which competes in Moto2 and previously in Moto3. Valentino Rossi announced on Thursday, August 5, 2021, that he would retire at the end of the 2021 MotoGP season. After his dominant run with Yamaha, Rossi faced some challenges in his career. He struggled to find the same success with Ducati in 2011 and 2012, finishing in seventh and sixth place respectively in the championship standings. However, Rossi's return to Yamaha in 2013 was a turning point in his career. He finished second in the championship that year, and although he has not won another championship since his dominant run in the mid-2000s, he has continued to be a competitive force in the sport. Rossi announced his retirement from full-time racing at the end of the 2021 season, after an illustrious career spanning more than two decades. Throughout his career, he won 9 Grand Prix World Championships and 115 races, making him one of the most successful riders in the history of the sport. Rossi's Impact on MotoGP Rossi's impact on the sport of MotoGP cannot be overstated. His skill, personality, and style have made him one of the most beloved riders of all time, and he has helped to bring the sport to a wider audience. His success on the track has also inspired a new generation of riders, many of whom cite Rossi as a major influence.
